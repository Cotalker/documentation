_Work orders_ for car maintenance are automatically created every 6,000 miles, thanks to [Sarah's preventive maintenance plan](/docs/products/workflows/preventive_plans/sample).

Below we see how her plan is carried out along the _work order_ workflow.

## Accept Work Order {#accept}

<div className="alert alert--primary">

The car has reached a bit more than 12,000 miles. It's time for its next maintenance check. 

Thomas, a maintenance contractor, receives the automatically generated _work order_. After viewing the details, he chooses to accept the job and fills out the corresponding form.

<img alt="create work order" className="img_sizing item shadow--tl" src={useBaseUrl('img/products/video_create_work_order(pm).gif')} />
<br/>

</div>
<br/>

## Checklist {#checklist}

<div className="alert alert--primary">

After accepting the work order, Thomas receives a checklist of items to inspect. Upon inspection, Thomas immediately sends back the checklist with the current state of the items.

<img alt="checklist" className="img_sizing item shadow--tl" src={useBaseUrl('img/products/video_preventive_wo_checklist.gif')} />

## Close Work Order {#close-wo}

<div className="alert alert--primary">

## Recepction and Feedback {#reception}

<div className="alert alert--primary">

Sarah receives Thomas' close work order request. She checks out the report and decides to approve the completed maintenance job.

<img alt="acceptance of work" className="img_sizing item shadow--tl" src={useBaseUrl('img/products/video_preventive_wo_acceptance.gif')} />
